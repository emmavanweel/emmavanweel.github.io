Choropleth map

Creating a comparison map of Sustainability Development Goals(SDG) indicators with QGIS on Esri online

The SDG indicators that are made into choropleth maps for comparison are 15.1.1 and 15.1.2. Indicator 15.1.1 contains the data of the forest area as a percentage of total land area and indicator 15.1.2 contains the data of the average proportion of Terrestrial Key Biodiversity Areas (KBAs) covered by protected areas. This second indicator has been chosen because forest areas are often rich in biodiversity. Therefore, many terrestrial KBAs are most likely located within forested regions because these areas tend to have high levels of endemic species and unique ecosystems that need protection to be conserved. A choropleth map is a type of map that uses different shades of colors to show the value of a specific variable across geographic areas. They provide a clear and simple visual representation of complex data and are therefore widely used. However, they must be designed carefully to avoid misleading viewers, especially by normalizing data to account for differences in area and population size. To make the choropleth maps, a clean world map first needed to be uploaded in QGIS. Then, a shape file of the data was downloaded from the UN SDG indicator meta database and uploaded to QGIS. Next, the data from the world map and the indicator had to be joined to make sure the values are coupled to the right countries. The joined data was then graduated and styled after which the word map and indicator were deactivated. Lastly, the projection was changed to ‘equal area’ for the right representation showing that the earth is round. The map was then printed and turned into a pdf after adding a legend.

These maps visualize the forest area as a percentage of total land area and the average proportion of Terrestrial Key Biodiversity Areas (KBAs) covered by protected areas. When comparing these maps, it is striking that the middle of Africa and Brazil have a lot of forest area but not a lot of KBAs. Europe on the other hand, doesn’t have a lot of forest area but a lot of KBAs.
